If you think about the market at all, you may have asked yourself at some time, “What determines stock prices?” Every TV dummy that you listen to on CNBC will tell you a one word answer, “Earnings”. And they’re wrong.

Carnivores don’t even ask that question. It’s the wrong question! We ask a different question…we ask “What determines stock price movement?” After all, we only care about stock price movement - as that is what will determine profit or loss in a trade, the only thing that really matters, right? And the answer to what determines stock price movement is more complex and multi-dimensional than something simple like “earnings”.

We postulate that the companies value today is irrelevant, particularly in relation to other stocks. Overvalued, undervalued, it doesn’t matter. Because…by whose definition? Some idiot on TV? Some analyst sitting in New York? Value isn’t determined by any of them. Value is determined by the combined energy of buying and selling of millions of people. Earnings? Earnings don’t matter. If earnings mattered, why are millions of people sitting with losing positions and saying to themselves, “Well, the company has great earnings, I wonder why my stupid ass is holding onto this stock now that it’s down 20% or 50% from where I bought it?
