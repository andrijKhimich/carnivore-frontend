Market Commentary and Featured Articles

Market Guidance

We say the action was constructive today because when the market opens down like this, pretty damn hard, and then sucks up the losses and goes positive in a lot of names, its not a bad thing, it’s a shake out. Think of all the market makers that took the stocks down, tripped all the stop loss orders, took in that stock, and then when the hedgies and others came in and started buying the “bargains” the market makers were unloading their positions at huge gains today.

That’s what we just saw happen. The Waterfall Pattern – A Market Action Theory
What is going to be more interesting is if this pattern happens again over the next couple days.
If it does then this will look like a waterfall in the charts. Open down hard, climb back a bit.
But each new opening is a bit lower than the one the day before.
That looks like a waterfall in the charts.
If that pattern develops, it becomes a day trader market.
Buy on the open (down hard) and sell near the close (after they climb up a bit.) That’s the only way to make money in the waterfall market.

Since we’re not day traders, that kind of keeps us out of the market, at least for new issues, and all we can do is hang onto the stocks that aren’t gong to get hit as hard. The other thing we can do is find some decent short positions and reverse the trade. Short at the close, cover in the AM when the market opens down hard. Also just another version of a day trade, so not something we really like to do.

BUT

It might be the only way to make money in December. Tomorrow will give us an indication. I might take a flyer today and short at the close to see if my theory is right. And so we did. Now we will see if we’re right.

Disclaimer and Explanation of How Results are Calculated

We have opted to represent the performance of our recommendations in a very simple way. We take every recommendation and keep a running total of the gains and losses. We take the gainers and we add them up and we subtract the losers for realized gains or losses. For example, if we recommend 5 trades, and they are +10%, -5%, -5%, -5% and +10%, we will show our track record as +5.0%. If we make multiple buys of a stock and sell them all at once, every “lot” is counted towards the gain or loss equally. For example, we make 10 buys of 100 shares of a stock over a week totaling 1,000 shares at different prices, then we sell 1000 in a block, each 100 share lot has a different gain or loss.

Each one is added to the gain or loss of the track record – we do not average the gain or loss on the position. With a winner this works to our favor but with a loser, it works equally against us. We started tracking and making recommendations on 3/18/2020 where our recommendation performance began at 0.00%. We assumed a $9.99 per trade commission cost in and out until 7-1-20. Since 7-1-20 we report results with no commissions as there are now several "no commission" brokerage options available to the general public.
